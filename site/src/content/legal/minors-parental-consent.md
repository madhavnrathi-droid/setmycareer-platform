# Children & Parental Consent Policy

SetMyCareer is a career-counselling service, and many of the people we help are students who are minors. This policy explains, in plain language, how we protect children, when we require a parent or guardian to give consent, what information we collect about a child and why, and how a parent or guardian can review, control or delete that information. It sits alongside our Privacy Policy and Terms of Service, and it controls wherever a child is involved.

## 1. Who this policy is for

This policy is for:

- **Parents and legal guardians** deciding whether to let a child use SetMyCareer, and who want to know exactly what we do with a child's information.
- **Young users** who want to understand what we ask their parent or guardian to approve, and what we will and will not do with their information.
- **Schools, colleges and organisations** that engage SetMyCareer for students, so they understand the consent obligations that apply.

Throughout this policy, "**child**", "**minor**" and "**young user**" mean a person who has not reached the age of adulthood that applies to them:

- In **India**, a "child" is anyone **under 18 years of age** (Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023).
- In the **United States**, our verification obligations are strongest for a child **under 13 years of age** (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act, "COPPA"); we also apply protections for older minors (13–17) as described below.

"**Parent**" means a parent or lawful guardian who is legally entitled to act for the child.

## 2. The core rule, in one line

**If the user is a minor, a parent or guardian must give verifiable consent before we collect the child's personal data, take payment, or start any assessment, session or coaching — and the parent or guardian, not the child, is the person who contracts with us.**

Everything else in this policy explains how that rule works in practice.

---

## 3. Why age matters to us

SetMyCareer offers, among other things, psychometric assessments, an AI career coach (including a live voice agent), recorded and transcribed live counselling sessions with human counsellors, and AI-generated career reports. These are guidance tools that involve collecting meaningful information about a young person — their interests, aptitudes, personality traits, and what they say in a session.

Because of that, the law in both India and the United States gives children special protection, and so do we. We treat a child's information more carefully than an adult's, we ask a parent or guardian to make the decisions, and we do **not** profile children for advertising.

## 5. What we do at sign-up: the age-gate

Before we collect anything more than the minimum needed to work out how old you are, we ask for a **date of birth (or age band)**. This is our "age-gate". Based on the answer, we route the user down one of three paths:

1. **Adult (18+ everywhere).** The user can proceed and consent for themselves, subject to our Terms of Service and Privacy Policy.
2. **Minor in India (under 18), or child in the US (under 13).** We **pause** and route to the **parental-consent flow** (Section 6) before collecting any further personal data, taking any payment, or starting any assessment, session or coaching.
3. **US minor aged 13–17.** The user may create a basic account, but we ask for a parent or guardian's involvement and consent before recorded sessions, purchases, or any processing that a state law reserves for adults. Where a school or organisation has engaged us for the student, Section 12 applies.

We do not knowingly collect more information than we need to run the age-gate until the correct consent is in place. We ask users to answer the date-of-birth question honestly; the exact microcopy we show is set out in Section 15.

## 6. The parental-consent flow (how a parent approves)

When the age-gate identifies a child, we collect the personal data needed to obtain and verify parental consent, and nothing more, until consent is given. The flow is:

### 6.1 Step 1 — We ask for a parent or guardian

We ask the young user (or the referring school) to give a parent or guardian's name and a contact channel (email address and/or mobile number). We tell the child that a parent or guardian needs to approve before they can continue.

### 6.2 Step 2 — We give the parent clear notice

We send the parent or guardian a **notice** (by email and/or SMS/WhatsApp [CONFIRM channels in use]) that clearly explains, in plain language:

- who we are and how to reach us;
- that their child wishes to use SetMyCareer;
- exactly **what personal data we would collect** and **why** (see Section 8);
- that assessments, sessions and coaching are **guidance, not clinical, medical or diagnostic** services, and are **not** an admission, employment, salary or outcome guarantee;
- that live counselling sessions are **recorded and transcribed** (audio/video, an AI transcript and counsellor notes), and that the family keeps the report, transcript and video;
- that the parent or guardian is the **contracting party** and will be asked to accept our Terms and this policy on the child's behalf;
- the parent's rights and how to exercise them (Sections 9 and 10); and
- a link to this policy and to our full Privacy Policy.

### 6.3 Step 3 — We verify that it is really the parent

We use a **verifiable** method appropriate to the sensitivity of what happens next. Depending on the activity, we use one or more of:

- **Verified OTP confirmation** — the parent confirms a one-time password sent to their own email and/or mobile, and affirmatively accepts the consent notice (used for account creation and assessments).
- **Payment-record verification** — where the parent makes a purchase, the transaction is completed on a payment instrument in the parent's control through our payment processor (Razorpay in India, and card processors for US users), which provides an additional signal that an adult is giving consent. We do **not** store full card details; our processor handles payment credentials.
- **Signed consent** — for higher-touch services (for example, recorded live counselling for a young child), the parent completes a consent form (electronic signature or uploaded signed form) and/or a verified consent call with our team.
- **Government-or-school-mediated confirmation** — where a school or organisation has engaged us (Section 12), consent may be collected and verified through the institution under a written agreement, together with parental notice.

We keep a record of the consent given (what was consented to, by whom, and when). [CONFIRM: which verification methods are enabled at launch, so this list matches the live product.]

### 6.4 Step 4 — Only then do we proceed

We begin collecting the child's assessment or session data, take any payment, and unlock the service **only after** verifiable parental consent is recorded. If consent is refused or not completed, we do not proceed, and we delete the limited contact data collected to run the flow, unless we are required to keep a minimal record that consent was declined.

### India (DPDP Act, 2023)

For **every user under 18** resident in or accessing SetMyCareer from India, we obtain the **verifiable consent of a parent or lawful guardian** before processing the child's personal data. The parent or guardian is the person who accepts our Terms and gives consent, and they may withdraw it at any time. Where a Consent Manager is used, the parent may give, manage and withdraw consent through that Consent Manager once the framework is operational. ### United States (COPPA — children under 13)

For **every user under 13** in the United States, we obtain **verifiable parental consent** before collecting, using or disclosing the child's personal information, consistent with COPPA. We give parents "direct notice" of our practices (Section 6.2), we use a verifiable consent method reasonably designed to ensure the person giving consent is the parent (Section 6.3), and we honour the parental rights in Section 10. We collect from a child only what is reasonably necessary for the service and do not condition a child's participation on disclosing more than is needed.

## 7. What we will never do with a child's information

Regardless of jurisdiction, for users we know to be children we do **not**:

- serve **targeted or behavioural advertising** to the child, or build an advertising profile of the child;
- carry out **behavioural tracking or monitoring** of the child for advertising or for any purpose not necessary to provide and safeguard the service;
- **sell or "share"** the child's personal information (as "sale" and "share" are defined under California law), or disclose it for cross-context behavioural advertising;
- use the child's data to **train third-party advertising models**; or
- require the child to give more information than is reasonably necessary to take part.

The DPDP Act specifically prohibits tracking, behavioural monitoring and targeted advertising directed at children, and COPPA and US state laws impose parallel restrictions. We build to the stricter of the rules that apply.

## 8. What child data we collect, and why

We collect only what we need to provide guidance and to keep the child safe. The table shows the main categories.

| What we collect | Why we collect it |
| --- | --- |
| **Age band / date of birth** | To run the age-gate and route to the correct consent path |
| **Parent/guardian contact details** (name, email, mobile) | To send notice, obtain and verify consent, and communicate about the child's account |
| **Child's basic profile** (name, grade/year, school [where relevant], email/phone as needed) | To create and operate the account and personalise guidance |
| **Assessment responses and results** (Big Five personality, RIASEC interests, aptitude) | To generate psychometric guidance and the career report; these are **guidance instruments, not clinical or diagnostic tests** |
| **Session recordings and derived records** — audio/video of live counselling, AI transcripts, and counsellor notes | To deliver the counselling session, produce the report and transcript the family keeps, and maintain a record of the guidance given |
| **AI coach interactions** (text chat and live voice) | To provide automated, AI-generated career guidance and to improve and safeguard the coach |
| **AI-generated career reports** | To give the child and parent structured career guidance (not an admission, employment, salary or outcome guarantee) |
| **Payment and transaction records** (held by the parent's payment instrument via our processor) | To take payment from the parent and meet accounting and legal obligations; we do not store full card numbers |
| **Wellbeing signals that may surface in a session** | Only to respond appropriately and signpost support — **SetMyCareer is not a mental-health treatment, crisis, emergency or medical service** (see Section 11) |

We do not use a child's assessment results, session content, or coach interactions to advertise to the child, and we do not sell or share them.

## 9. India — the child's and parent's rights (DPDP Act, 2023)

Where the DPDP Act applies, the parent or lawful guardian, acting for the child as the Data Principal, may:

- **Access** a summary of the child's personal data we process and how we process it;
- **Correct, complete or update** the child's personal data;
- **Erase** the child's personal data where it is no longer needed for the purpose it was collected for or where consent is withdrawn (subject to any record we must keep by law);
- **Withdraw consent** at any time, as easily as it was given — after which we stop the relevant processing, though this does not affect processing already carried out lawfully;
- **Nominate** another individual to exercise these rights if the parent is unable to (for example, in the event of death or incapacity);
- **Raise a grievance** with our Grievance Officer (Section 13) and, if unsatisfied, escalate to the **Data Protection Board of India**.

To exercise any of these, contact us using the details in Section 13. We respond within the timelines in Section 13 and applicable law.

## 10. United States — parental rights (COPPA and state laws)

### United States (California residents — CCPA/CPRA) and other state privacy laws

A parent or guardian, or the minor where the law allows, may:

- **Know** what personal information we have collected about the child and how we use and disclose it;
- **Access** a copy of that information;
- **Correct** inaccurate information;
- **Delete** the child's information (subject to limited legal exceptions);
- **Opt out of any "sale" or "sharing"** of personal information and of targeted advertising — noting that we **do not** sell or share children's information or use it for targeted advertising in the first place;
- **Limit the use of sensitive personal information**; and
- **Not be discriminated against** for exercising these rights.

Under California's CPRA, personal information of a consumer under 16 may not be "sold" or "shared" without opt-in consent — from the parent for a child under 13, and from the minor for a 13–15 year old — and, as stated, we do not sell or share children's data at all. A parent may use an **authorised agent** to submit requests, and we may verify the request and the agent's authority. Residents of Virginia (VCDPA), Colorado (CPA), Connecticut, Texas and other states with comparable laws have similar rights, including heightened protections for known children's data; we honour these at a general level and treat known-child data as sensitive.

### COPPA parental rights (children under 13)

A parent of a child under 13 may at any time:

- **Review** the personal information we have collected from their child;
- **Refuse to permit further collection or use** of the child's information;
- **Direct us to delete** the child's information; and
- **Revoke consent** previously given.

If a parent revokes consent or asks us to delete the child's information, we will stop collecting, using and disclosing that information and delete it, and we will explain if that means the child can no longer use part of the service. To make any of these requests, contact us using the details in Section 13.

**SetMyCareer is a career-guidance service. It is not a mental-health treatment, crisis, emergency or medical service, and it is not a substitute for professional help.** Our counsellors and AI coach do not provide diagnosis or therapy.

Where a school, college or organisation engages SetMyCareer to provide assessments, sessions or reports to students, we act under a written agreement with that institution. In those arrangements:

- we process student data only for the agreed educational and career-guidance purposes, and not for advertising;
- parental notice and consent (Section 6) still apply for minors, and may be collected and verified through the institution; and
