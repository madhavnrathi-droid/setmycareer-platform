# Acceptable Use Policy

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") sets out what you may and may not do when you use SetMyCareer. It exists to keep the platform safe, lawful and useful for everyone — students (including minors), working professionals, counsellors, domain experts, and our school and organisation partners.

This AUP is part of, and should be read with, our Terms of Service, Privacy Policy and Disclaimer. If you break this AUP, we may act as described in section 10.

SetMyCareer is a service of **Loratis SetMyCareer.Net India Private Limited** ("SetMyCareer", "we", "us", "our") [CONFIRM exact registered name; CIN: to be confirmed], registered office at No. 14/595, 1st Floor, Nanjappa Reddy Layout, Koramangala 8th Block, Bengaluru, Karnataka 560095, India.

**Last updated: 8 July 2026**

## 1. Who this policy applies to

This AUP applies to **everyone who accesses or uses SetMyCareer** in any capacity, through setmycareer.com or the SetMyCareer web app — including clients and their parents or guardians, independent-contractor counsellors ("navigators"), marketplace domain experts, B2B/institutional users, and any visitor. In this AUP, "content" means anything you submit, upload, post, share, record, transcribe, generate or transmit through the platform, and "you" means the person or organisation using the service.

## 2. The basic rule

Use SetMyCareer **lawfully, honestly, respectfully and only for its intended purpose** — getting or providing genuine career guidance. Do not use it to harm others, to break the law, to damage or misuse the platform, or in any way this AUP prohibits.

## 3. Prohibited conduct

You must not do any of the following, and must not help, encourage or permit anyone else to do them.

### 3.1 Illegal or harmful use

- Use the platform for any unlawful purpose, or in violation of any applicable law, regulation, court order, or the rules of any regulator, in India, the United States or anywhere your activity has effect.
- Use the platform to plan, promote, facilitate or carry out any illegal, fraudulent, deceptive or harmful activity.
- Do anything that could damage, disable, overburden, or impair the platform, our systems, or another user's use of the service.

### 3.2 Harassment, abuse and harmful content

- Harass, threaten, bully, stalk, defame, demean or intimidate anyone — **including our counsellors, domain experts and staff** — whether during a session, in chat, in reviews, or by any other means.
- Post, upload or transmit content that is unlawful, hateful, discriminatory, obscene, sexually explicit, violent, or that incites violence or hatred against any person or group.
- Engage in sexual advances, sexually explicit conduct, or any inappropriate behaviour towards a counsellor, expert, staff member or another user, in a session or otherwise.

### 3.3 Protection of minors

- Solicit, groom, sexually exploit, endanger, or attempt to make inappropriate contact with any minor.
- Produce, upload, request or share any content that sexually exploits or abuses a minor (**child sexual abuse material is absolutely prohibited and will be reported to law-enforcement authorities**).
- Use another person's, including a child's, account or identity, or provide a service to a minor outside the parental-consent and safeguarding processes described in our Privacy Policy and Terms.

### 3.4 Impersonation and misrepresentation

- Impersonate any person or organisation, or falsely state or imply an affiliation with, or endorsement by, any person, SetMyCareer, or any counsellor, expert, school or company.
- Create an account using false information, register on behalf of someone else without authority, or misrepresent your identity, age, qualifications or credentials (this is especially important for counsellors and domain experts, who must be truthful about their certifications and experience).

### 3.5 Privacy and other people's data

- Collect, upload, store, share or otherwise process another person's personal data **without that person's valid consent** and a lawful basis to do so.
- Share, publish or expose a counsellor's, expert's or other user's personal information, session content, contact details or private communications without authorisation ("doxxing" is prohibited).
- Record, screenshot, copy or redistribute a session, transcript, or any personal or confidential information beyond what your own account is entitled to, or in breach of applicable recording-consent laws.

### 3.6 Intellectual property and content

- Upload, post or share content that infringes any copyright, trademark, patent, trade secret, moral right, publicity right or other intellectual-property or proprietary right.
- Copy, reproduce, republish, distribute, publicly display or create derivative works from SetMyCareer's content, software, assessments, question banks, methodologies or branding, except as expressly permitted.
- **Resell, sublicense, rent, redistribute or commercially exploit reports, assessments, transcripts, session recordings or any other SetMyCareer materials**, or make them available to third parties, without our prior written permission. Your report, transcript and video are provided for **your own personal use**.

### 3.7 Scraping, automated access and interference

- Access the platform using any bot, spider, crawler, scraper, or other automated means, or scrape, harvest, index or data-mine any content, user data, expert profiles or listings, except with our prior written permission.
- Use the platform to build, train or improve any competing product, dataset or machine-learning model, or to benchmark it for a competitor.
- Circumvent, disable or interfere with rate limits, access controls, CAPTCHAs, or any security or authentication feature.

### 3.8 Security, reverse engineering and malware

- Reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, or attempt to derive the source code, structure or underlying ideas of the platform, except to the limited extent that applicable law expressly permits despite this restriction.
- Introduce, upload or transmit any virus, worm, trojan, ransomware, malware, or other malicious or harmful code, or any file designed to disrupt, damage or gain unauthorised access to any system or data.
- Probe, scan or test the vulnerability of our systems, breach or attempt to breach our security or authentication measures, or access any account, data, server or network without authorisation.

### 3.9 Payments and platform integrity

- Circumvent, manipulate or evade our payment, pricing, subscription, credit or booking systems — for example by using stolen or unauthorised payment methods, filing fraudulent chargebacks, exploiting refund or coupon mechanisms in bad faith, or arranging to take a paid marketplace relationship "off-platform" to avoid fees where our Terms prohibit it.
- Create multiple or fake accounts to abuse trials, promotions or referral rewards, or to evade a suspension or ban.
- Post fake, incentivised or manipulated reviews or ratings, or otherwise game the marketplace.

### 3.10 Spam and unsolicited marketing

- Send spam, chain messages, pyramid schemes or unsolicited commercial communications through or using the platform.
- Use contact details obtained through SetMyCareer to market to, recruit or solicit users, counsellors or experts in breach of their consent, our Terms, or applicable law (including India's TRAI/DND framework and U.S. anti-spam rules).

## 4. Content standards

All content you provide must be accurate, lawful, your own (or properly licensed), and appropriate for a professional career-guidance platform used by minors and adults alike. You are responsible for your content and for keeping a backup of anything important to you. We may, but are not obliged to, review, moderate, refuse, remove or disable access to content that we reasonably believe breaches this AUP or the law.

## 5. Sessions, recording and conduct

Live video and voice counselling sessions are **recorded and transcribed** (audio/video, AI transcript and counsellor notes), as described in our Privacy Policy and Terms, and clients keep their report, transcript and video. When you take part in a session you must:

- behave professionally and respectfully towards the counsellor or expert and any other participant;
- not record, capture, or redistribute the session yourself beyond your own permitted copy, and not in breach of applicable all-party recording-consent laws; and
- not use the session to pursue any prohibited conduct in section 3.

Counsellors and domain experts must additionally deliver services honestly and competently, protect client confidentiality, follow our safeguarding rules (particularly for minors), and not solicit clients off-platform in breach of their contract with us.

## 6. Reporting abuse and violations

If you see content or behaviour that breaks this AUP — including harassment, a safety concern, a threat to a minor, impersonation, fraud, an intellectual-property complaint, or a security vulnerability — please report it to us promptly:

- **Email:** grievance@setmycareer.com [CONFIRM] (or info@setmycareer.com [CONFIRM])
- **Phone:** +91-9108510058 (Mon–Sun, 09:00–20:00 IST)

Please include enough detail for us to investigate: what happened, where (URLs, account names, session references), when, and any supporting evidence such as screenshots. We take reports seriously and, where a report concerns a minor's safety or an imminent risk of harm, we prioritise it. **If someone is in immediate danger, contact emergency services first** — 112 in India, 911 in the United States (see the crisis resources in our Disclaimer).

## 7. Illegal-content and takedown requests (intermediary obligations)

Where SetMyCareer acts as an intermediary, we will remove or disable access to unlawful content in accordance with applicable law once we have valid knowledge of it — including on receipt of a court order or a lawful government direction, and, in the case of certain content (such as non-consensual intimate imagery), within the timelines the law requires. See our Grievance Officer details in section 11.

## 8. Consequences of breaking this policy

If you breach this AUP, or we reasonably believe you have, we may take any action we consider appropriate and proportionate, including:

- issuing a warning;
- removing, editing or disabling access to offending content;
- limiting, suspending or **terminating** your access, account, listing or specific features, with or without notice depending on severity;
- withholding, cancelling or reversing payouts, or reversing transactions, in cases of fraud or abuse;
- taking steps to prevent you from creating new accounts;
- pursuing any legal remedy available to us, including recovering losses and costs; and
- **reporting your activity to law-enforcement, regulatory or other competent authorities**, and cooperating with them, where we are required to or reasonably consider it necessary — for example in cases involving threats to safety, exploitation of minors, fraud, or serious illegality.

For serious breaches (such as those endangering a minor, involving illegal content, fraud, or a security attack) we may act immediately and without prior notice. Our decision to act, or not to act, in any instance does not waive our right to act in another.

## 9. Jurisdiction-specific notes

### India

For users in India, this AUP operates alongside our obligations as an intermediary under the Information Technology Act, 2000 and the Information Technology (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules, 2021, including the requirement to publish these rules and to maintain a Grievance Officer (see section 11). Prohibited content includes anything the law bars us or you from hosting or sharing. We may disclose information and remove content as required by Indian law, court orders, or lawful government requests. This AUP is governed by the laws of India, with the courts at Bengaluru, Karnataka having jurisdiction.

### United States

For users in the United States, misuse of the platform may also violate federal and state law, including the FTC Act (unfair or deceptive practices), the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) for children under 13, computer-misuse and anti-fraud statutes (such as the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act), state recording-consent laws (many states require the consent of all parties to record a conversation — see section 5), and intellectual-property law. We will respond to valid intellectual-property complaints (including notices under the Digital Millennium Copyright Act) and cooperate with lawful requests from U.S. authorities. Nothing here limits your non-waivable rights under applicable U.S. consumer-protection or privacy law.
