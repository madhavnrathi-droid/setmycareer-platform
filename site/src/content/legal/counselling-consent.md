## Quick summary (the short version)

- **This is career guidance, not therapy or medical care.** Our counsellors and coaches help you with education, careers and skills. They do not diagnose, treat or prescribe.
- **Your session is recorded.** Live sessions are recorded (audio and video), transcribed by AI, and your counsellor takes notes. You receive your own copies.
- **We use AI.** An AI career coach, a live AI voice agent, AI transcription and AI-generated reports may be used. AI can make mistakes; a qualified human counsellor is responsible for the final recommendations.

## 1. What a SetMyCareer session is

SetMyCareer provides **career counselling, guidance and assessment**. Depending on what you have chosen, a session may involve:

- a live **video or voice counselling session** with a certified human counsellor (a "navigator") or a paid, independent **domain expert**;
- an **AI career coach** — text chat and/or a live AI **voice agent** — that gives automated, AI-generated guidance;
- discussion of your **psychometric assessment** results (for example, Big Five personality, RIASEC interests, and aptitude); and
- an **AI-generated career report** with guidance and suggested next steps.

The purpose is to help you understand your strengths, interests and options and to make informed decisions about education, careers and skills.

## 2. What a SetMyCareer session is **not**

This is the most important thing to understand before you begin.

- SetMyCareer is **not a medical, psychological, psychiatric or therapeutic service.** A session is **not** therapy, counselling for a mental-health condition, medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.
- Our **assessments are guidance instruments, not clinical, medical or diagnostic tests.** They indicate tendencies and preferences to support career conversations. They do **not** measure, diagnose or rule out any medical or psychological condition, and they must not be used or read as if they do.
- Our counsellors, navigators and experts are **career professionals, not licensed clinicians, doctors or mental-health practitioners** (unless a specific individual separately holds and is engaging you in such a capacity, which SetMyCareer does not provide or supervise).
- **No outcome is promised.** Guidance and reports are for your information and decision-making only. We do **not** guarantee any admission, examination result, job, internship, promotion, salary, visa or any other outcome. Decisions and their consequences remain yours.
- SetMyCareer is **not a crisis, emergency or suicide-prevention service** (see Section 8).

If, during a session, it appears that you would benefit from medical, psychological or mental-health support, your counsellor may gently suggest that you consider speaking to a qualified professional and may share the resources in Section 8. This is a signpost, not treatment, and not a diagnosis.

## 3. Recording, transcription and notes

To deliver the service, keep a reliable record, support quality and safety, and give you materials you can keep, **your live sessions are recorded.**

### 3.1 What we capture

| What | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Audio and video recording** | The full audio and, for video sessions, the video of the session. |
| **AI transcript** | A written transcript generated automatically by AI speech-to-text from the recording. |
| **Counsellor notes** | Notes written by your counsellor during or after the session (observations, suggested next steps, action points). |
| **Session outputs** | Your career report, assessment results and any resources shared with you. |

### 3.2 Why we do it

- To **provide the service** you booked and to prepare your report, transcript and follow-up.
- To give **you your own copies** to revisit and act on.
- For **quality, training and supervision** of counsellors, and to improve the service.
- For **safety** — so there is an accurate record if a wellbeing or safety concern arises.
- To keep records we are **required or permitted to keep by law**, and to handle any dispute, grievance or claim.

### 3.3 Who can access recordings, transcripts and notes

- **You** — you receive and keep your report, transcript and session video.
- **Your counsellor / navigator / expert** for that session, to deliver and follow up on your session.
- **A limited number of authorised SetMyCareer staff** (for example, quality, safety, grievance handling, technical support) on a need-to-know basis.
- **Trusted service providers (processors)** who host our systems and run transcription and AI features on our behalf, under confidentiality and data-protection obligations, and only to provide those services to us.
- For a **minor**, the parent or guardian who gave consent may also be given access (see Section 10).
- **Others only where the law requires it or Section 7 applies** (for example, a valid legal order, or a serious safety risk).

We do **not** sell your recordings, transcripts or notes, and we do not use your session content for advertising.

## 4. Your consent to recording

Recording is **built in** to live sessions and is necessary to provide them. By continuing, you consent to the audio/video recording, AI transcription and note-taking described in Section 3.

### India (single-party consent)

Under Indian law, your consent to recording the session in which you take part is sufficient. **By ticking the recording consent box, you give that consent.** If you do not wish to be recorded, please tell us before the session — in most cases a live counselling session cannot proceed without recording, and we will help you find a suitable alternative (for example, an assessment-only or text-based option) where available.

### United States (all-party / two-party consent)

Some US states require the consent of **everyone** on the call before it may be recorded ("all-party" or "two-party" consent). If you are in the United States, **we ask for your express consent to recording** and you should treat the recording as covering all participants. Please only invite or bring another person onto the session if they also agree to being recorded. If any participant does not consent, tell your counsellor before the session so recording can be addressed or the session rescheduled. **Do not make your own separate recording** of a session, or record any other participant, without their consent, as this may be unlawful in your state.

## 5. Use of Artificial Intelligence (AI disclosure)

We want you to know clearly where AI is involved, because part of the guidance you receive may be generated by software rather than a person.

### 5.1 Where AI is used

- **AI career coach (text chat)** — an automated assistant that answers questions and gives career guidance.
- **AI voice agent** — a live, automated voice that can speak with you like a coach. It is **not a human**, even though it may sound like one, and it will be identified as automated.
- **AI transcription** — your session audio is turned into a written transcript automatically.
- **AI-generated reports** — your career report is drafted with the help of AI based on your inputs, assessment results and session.

### 5.2 What this means for you

- **AI can make mistakes.** AI-generated guidance, transcripts and reports may be **incomplete, inaccurate or out of date**, and may occasionally produce content that is wrong or does not fit your situation. Please use judgement and check anything important.
- **AI does not replace a professional.** AI output is **guidance, not advice** on which you should rely without your own consideration, and it is not medical, legal, financial or psychological advice.
- **A human owns the final recommendation.** Where a session involves a human counsellor, that **qualified human counsellor is responsible for the final career recommendations** given to you, and reviews or stands behind the guidance. Purely AI-generated output (for example, from the AI coach alone) is automated and has not necessarily been reviewed by a counsellor before you see it.
- **Please tell us if something looks wrong.** If a transcript or report contains an error, you can ask us to correct it (see Section 6).

We do not use AI to make any legally significant or similarly important decision about you without a person being involved where required.

## 6. Your data and privacy rights

The recordings, transcript, notes, assessment results and report are **personal data**, and some of it may be **sensitive** (for example, content about your wellbeing). We handle it in line with our **Privacy Policy**, which explains our legal bases, how we protect the information, international transfers, and how to exercise your rights. In summary you may, subject to law:

- **access** a copy of your information and ask how it is used;
- **correct** information that is wrong (including errors in an AI transcript or report);
- **erase** information, or **withdraw consent**, subject to records we must keep by law;
- **restrict or object** to certain uses; and
- **complain** to us and to the relevant regulator.

### India (DPDP Act, 2023)

As a **Data Principal**, you have rights of access, correction, completion, updating and erasure, the right to grievance redressal, and the right to nominate another person to exercise your rights in the event of death or incapacity. You may withdraw consent as easily as you gave it. You may escalate an unresolved grievance to the **Data Protection Board of India**. For anyone **under 18**, processing is based on **verifiable parental/guardian consent** (see Section 10). Contact our **Grievance Officer / Data Protection Officer** in Section 12.

### United States (California residents — CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident you have the right to **know, access, correct and delete** your personal information, to **opt out of the "sale" or "sharing"** of personal information, to **limit the use of Sensitive Personal Information**, and to **non-discrimination** for exercising your rights. You may use an **authorised agent**. We **do not sell** your session content and do not use it for cross-context behavioural advertising. To exercise these rights use the details in Section 12 or the "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" and privacy-rights options on our website. Residents of other US states (for example, Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut and Texas) have broadly similar rights, which we honour where applicable.

### EU / UK visitors (GDPR / UK GDPR — brief note)

If you are in the EU/UK, we process your information on lawful bases such as **consent**, **performance of a contract** and our **legitimate interests**, and you have rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, objection and portability, and the right to complain to your supervisory authority. Special-category data (such as wellbeing content) is handled with additional safeguards and, where required, your explicit consent. Contact details are in Section 12.

## 7. Confidentiality and its limits

We treat what you share as **confidential** and share it only as described in this notice and our Privacy Policy. There are a few **important exceptions** where we may need to disclose information **without your consent**, limited to what is necessary:

- **Risk of serious harm.** If we reasonably believe there is a serious and imminent risk to your life or safety, or to the safety of another person (including a child or vulnerable person), we may share information with people who can help, such as an emergency service, a designated safeguarding contact, or — for a minor — a parent or guardian.
- **Legal obligation.** Where we are required to disclose by law, regulation, court order, or a valid, lawful request from an authority.
- **Establishing or defending legal claims,** or handling a grievance, complaint or dispute.
- **Safeguarding of children and vulnerable persons,** in line with applicable law.

Wherever it is safe and lawful to do so, we will try to tell you first, share only the minimum necessary, and act proportionately.

## 9. Voluntariness and withdrawing your consent

- **Taking part is your choice.** You can decline to answer any question, ask to pause or stop the session, or end it at any time, without having to give a reason.
- **You can withdraw your consent** to recording, transcription, AI features or further processing at any time. To do so, tell your counsellor during a session, or contact us using Section 12.
- **What withdrawal affects.** Withdrawing consent applies from the time you tell us and does not undo processing already carried out lawfully before then. Because live recording is necessary to deliver a live session, withdrawing recording consent may mean a live session cannot go ahead or must end; we will help you find a suitable alternative where one exists. Withdrawal may also affect our ability to provide certain outputs (for example, a transcript of a session you asked us not to record).
- **Deletion.** You can ask us to delete your recordings, transcript, notes and report (see Section 6). We will do so unless we are required or permitted by law to keep certain records.
- Withdrawing consent will **not** affect the crisis resources above or any step we must take under Section 7 to protect safety or meet a legal obligation.

## 10. Minors (people under 18)

We provide guidance to students, **including minors**. Where the client is a minor, participation requires the consent of a **parent or legal guardian**, and this Notice must be read and agreed by that parent or guardian on the minor's behalf.

- **India (DPDP Act, 2023):** for anyone **under 18**, we obtain **verifiable parental/guardian consent** before processing the child's personal data, and we do not undertake tracking, behavioural monitoring or targeted advertising directed at children.
- **United States (COPPA):** for children **under 13**, we obtain **verifiable parental consent** before collecting personal information, in line with COPPA.
- The consenting parent or guardian may access the minor's recordings, transcript, notes and report, and may exercise the rights in Section 6 on the minor's behalf.
