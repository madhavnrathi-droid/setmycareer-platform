## 2. What are cookies and similar technologies?

**Cookies** are small text files stored on your device (computer, tablet or phone) when you visit a website. They let the site recognise your device on your next visit or as you move between pages.

We also use technologies that behave like cookies, and we treat them under the same categories and controls in this policy:

- **Local storage and session storage** — larger data stores in your browser used to keep the app responsive (for example, to hold your session, remember a draft, or cache parts of the interface).
- **IndexedDB** — an in-browser database the web app may use to store larger, structured data locally (for example, cached content so the app loads faster).
- **Pixels / web beacons** — tiny transparent images or code snippets used to tell whether a page or email was opened, or to trigger analytics or advertising tags.
- **Software Development Kits (SDKs) and tags** — code included in the app that provides a feature (such as payments or analytics) and may read or write cookies or local storage.
- **Device and connection identifiers** — where a feature needs to recognise your device or secure your session (for example, fraud-prevention signals used by our payment processor).

In this policy, "**cookies**" means all of the above unless we say otherwise.

### First-party vs third-party cookies

- **First-party cookies** are set by SetMyCareer directly and are used to run the Platform.
- **Third-party cookies** are set by companies that provide services to us — for example, our payment processor, analytics provider or advertising platforms. Those third parties handle the data they collect under their own privacy and cookie notices. We list the main third parties in the table in Section 5 and link to their notices where we can.

### Session vs persistent cookies

- **Session cookies** last only for your current visit and are deleted when you close your browser.
- **Persistent cookies** remain on your device for a set period (or until you delete them) so we can remember you, your preferences and your consent choices on future visits.

## 3. Why we use cookies

We use cookies to:

1. **Run the Platform and keep it secure** — sign you in, keep you signed in, route you to the right console (client portal, counsellor console or admin), protect against fraud and abuse, and process payments.
2. **Remember your choices** — such as your language, region (India/US), time zone, interface settings and, critically, your cookie and privacy choices.
3. **Understand and improve the Platform** — see which features (assessments, the AI career coach, reports, expert bookings) are used and where people run into problems, so we can improve them.
4. **Support our marketing** — where you allow it, measure the performance of our campaigns and show you relevant information about SetMyCareer.

We do **not** use cookies to make automated decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects about you. Our assessments and AI-generated reports are **guidance instruments**, not clinical, medical or diagnostic tools, and are explained in our Privacy Policy and Terms.

## 4. The cookie categories we use

We group cookies into four categories. You can control each optional category independently (see Section 6).

### 4.1 Strictly necessary (always on)

These cookies are essential for the Platform to work. They enable core functions such as signing in, keeping your session secure, load-balancing, remembering items in a flow (for example, a booking or checkout in progress), and recording your cookie/consent choices. Because the Platform cannot function without them, they do not require consent and **cannot be switched off** through our banner. If you block them in your browser, parts of the Platform will not work.

*Examples:* authentication/session token; OTP login state; CSRF/security token; consent-state cookie; load-balancer routing; Razorpay payment and fraud-prevention cookies during checkout.

### 4.2 Functional / preferences (optional)

These cookies remember choices you make to give you a better, more personal experience — for example, your language, region, time zone, or whether you have dismissed a notice. Switching these off means the Platform may not remember your preferences.

*Examples:* language and locale preference; India/US region setting; UI/theme preferences; "hide this tip" state.

### 4.3 Analytics / performance (optional)

These cookies help us understand how the Platform is used — which pages and features are visited, how long tasks take, and where errors occur — so we can improve reliability and design. We aim to use analytics in an aggregated and pseudonymised way. Switching these off means we get less insight into how to improve, but the Platform will still work.

*Examples:* product analytics events and session identifiers; page-performance and error monitoring; feature-usage measurement.

### 4.4 Marketing / advertising (optional)

These cookies and pixels help us measure our marketing and, where permitted, show you relevant information about SetMyCareer on other websites and platforms. They may be set by advertising partners and can involve **"sharing"** of certain identifiers with those partners. Switching these off means you will still see general ads elsewhere, but they will be less tailored, and we will not use these technologies to build a marketing profile of you.

*Examples:* Google Ads / remarketing tags; Meta (Facebook/Instagram) pixel; LinkedIn Insight Tag; conversion-measurement pixels.

> **We do not sell your personal information for money.** Some marketing/advertising cookies may nonetheless count as a "sale" or "sharing" for cross-context behavioural advertising under California and other US state laws. You can opt out — see Section 7.

## 6. How to control cookies on the Platform

You are always in control of optional cookies. The Platform provides an in-product **consent banner** and a persistent **"Cookie settings"** control.

- **On your first visit**, our banner tells you we use cookies and lets you **Accept all**, **Reject all** (non-essential), or **Manage preferences** to switch categories on or off individually.
- **At any time afterwards**, open **"Cookie settings"** (in the footer and in your account settings) to change your choices. Your changes take effect straight away for future cookie-setting, and we will stop setting cookies in any category you turn off.
- **We remember your choice** using the strictly necessary `smc_consent` cookie, and we re-ask periodically or when this policy materially changes. Clearing your browser storage will delete this record, so the banner will appear again.
- **Withdrawing consent is as easy as giving it.** Turning a category off in "Cookie settings" is a one-step action, the same as turning it on. Withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of anything done before you withdrew it.

## 7. Your regional rights and controls

Your controls are the same on the surface (the banner and "Cookie settings"), but the **legal default differs** by region.

### India (DPDP Act, 2023)

Under the **Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023**, we rely on your **consent** for all non-essential cookies. This means:

- **Opt-in by default.** We do **not** set functional, analytics or marketing cookies until you agree through the banner. Only strictly necessary cookies run before you choose.
- **Clear notice and specific purposes.** The banner and this policy tell you what each category is for, in plain language, in English [CONFIRM whether other Eighth Schedule languages are offered].
- **Withdraw any time.** You can withdraw consent for any category through "Cookie settings" as easily as you gave it.
- **Grievances.** If you are unhappy with how we handle cookies or your data, contact our Grievance Officer / Data Protection Officer (Section 11). You may also escalate to the **Data Protection Board of India**.
- **Children.** For users we know to be **under 18**, we require **verifiable consent from a parent or lawful guardian** and do not set marketing/advertising cookies for them (Section 9).

### United States (California residents — CCPA/CPRA)

Under the **California Consumer Privacy Act, as amended by the CPRA**, certain analytics and marketing cookies may involve a **"sale"** or **"sharing"** (for cross-context behavioural advertising) of personal information, even though **we do not sell your data for money**. You have the right to opt out. This means:

- **Notice + opt-out.** In the US, non-essential cookies may run subject to your right to opt out. We provide a **"Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information"** link and a **"Limit the Use of My Sensitive Personal Information"** link (in the footer and in "Cookie settings").
- **Global Privacy Control (GPC).** We treat a valid **GPC** signal from your browser as a request to opt out of sale/sharing for that browser or device. See Section 8.
- **Right to know, delete, correct.** You can ask what personal information we hold, and ask us to delete or correct it, as described in our Privacy Policy.
- **Sensitive personal information.** We do not use sensitive personal information to infer characteristics for advertising; you can further limit its use through the link above.
- **No discrimination.** We will not deny you the service, charge you a different price, or give you a lower quality of service because you exercised these rights.
- **Authorised agents.** You may use an authorised agent to submit opt-out requests, as set out in our Privacy Policy.
- **Children.** For users we know to be **under 13**, we obtain **verifiable parental consent** under **COPPA** and do not set advertising cookies for them (Section 9). We do not sell or share the personal information of consumers we know to be under 16 without opt-in consent.

**Other US states.** Residents of states such as **Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut and Texas** (among others) have comparable rights, including to opt out of targeted advertising and the sale of personal data, and to have universal opt-out signals like GPC honoured. We extend the same controls to you. See our Privacy Policy for state-specific detail.

### European Economic Area & United Kingdom (brief note)

If you visit from the EEA or UK, we rely on your **consent** (GDPR/UK GDPR and the ePrivacy/PECR rules) for all non-essential cookies, set only after you opt in through the banner. Our **lawful basis** for strictly necessary cookies is our legitimate interest in providing a secure, working service; for all other categories it is your consent, which you can withdraw at any time. You have rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, objection and portability, and can complain to your local supervisory authority. ## 8. Global Privacy Control (GPC) and Do-Not-Track

- **Global Privacy Control (GPC).** GPC is a browser or extension setting that automatically tells websites you want to opt out of the sale/sharing of your personal information. Where you visit from a US state that recognises GPC, we treat a valid GPC signal as a request to opt out of "sale"/"sharing" and to turn off marketing/advertising cookies for that browser or device. Because GPC is tied to your browser, you may need to set it on each browser and device you use, and clearing your cookies may reset our record of it.
- **Do-Not-Track (DNT).** Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" setting. There is still no common industry standard for how to respond to DNT, so — like most services — we do **not** currently respond to DNT signals. We do, however, honour GPC as described above, and you can always control cookies through our banner and your browser.

## 10. Controlling cookies through your browser

Separately from our banner, you can control cookies through your browser. Most browsers let you see what cookies are stored, delete them, block third-party cookies, and block all cookies. Popular browsers publish instructions:

- **Google Chrome**, **Apple Safari**, **Mozilla Firefox**, **Microsoft Edge** — see each browser's "Privacy" or "Cookies and site data" settings.
- To clear local storage, session storage and IndexedDB, use your browser's "Clear browsing data" / "site data" controls.

**Please note:** if you block or delete **strictly necessary** cookies, core features — signing in, staying signed in, and paying — will not work. Deleting cookies also removes our record of your consent, so our banner will appear again on your next visit.

You can also learn about advertising controls at industry opt-out pages such as the Digital Advertising Alliance (`optout.aboutads.info`), the Network Advertising Initiative (`optout.networkadvertising.org`) and, in Europe, Your Online Choices (`youronlinechoices.eu`) [CONFIRM links current]. These are third-party tools that we do not control.
