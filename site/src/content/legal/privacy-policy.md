# Privacy Policy

Your privacy matters to us. This Privacy Policy explains, in plain language, what personal data we collect when you use SetMyCareer, why we collect it, who we share it with, how long we keep it, how we protect it, and the rights and choices you have. Please read it alongside our Terms of Service and Cookie Policy.

**Last updated: 8 July 2026**

SetMyCareer is a career-counselling and assessment service. Along the way we handle some sensitive information — your psychometric assessment responses, recordings and transcripts of your counselling sessions, and your conversations with our AI career coach. We have written this policy to be genuinely readable, but also complete: if you only read one section, read section 8 (Your rights) and section 9 (Children and minors).

## 2. Scope of this policy

This policy applies to personal data we process when you:

- browse or interact with setmycareer.com;
- create an account and use the SetMyCareer web app as a **client** (student, parent/guardian or working professional);
- take our psychometric **assessments** (Big Five personality, RIASEC interests, aptitude);
- chat with, or speak to, our **AI career coach** (text and live voice);
- attend live **video/voice counselling sessions** with our counsellors or domain experts;
- purchase packages, assessments or sessions;
- apply to work with us as a **counsellor ("navigator")** or **domain expert**; or
- represent a **school, college or organisation** that engages us (B2B).

It does **not** cover the privacy practices of third-party websites, apps or services we link to, or the independent practices of counsellors and experts acting outside the scope of the Services. It also does not replace any separate consent notice, informed-consent form or session-recording notice we present to you at the point of collection — those add detail specific to that activity.

### An important note on what SetMyCareer is not

SetMyCareer provides career guidance. Our assessments are **guidance instruments, not clinical, medical or diagnostic tests**, and our AI coach and reports produce **guidance, not a guarantee** of any admission, job, salary or other outcome. Wellbeing topics sometimes surface in counselling, but **SetMyCareer is not a mental-health treatment, crisis, emergency or medical service**. If you are in distress or in an emergency, please use the crisis resources in section 14.

## 3. The personal data we collect

We collect personal data (a) that you give us directly, (b) that is generated automatically when you use the Services, and (c) that we receive from third parties (for example, a school that enrols you, or a payment processor that confirms a payment). Some of this data is **sensitive** — we flag it below and give it extra protection.

### 3.1 Identity and account data
Name; username and password (stored only in hashed/encrypted form); date of birth and **age**; gender (optional); profile photo (optional); the account type you hold (client, counsellor, expert, admin) and your account settings.

### 3.2 Contact data
Email address; mobile/phone number; postal address or city/country; and details you provide when you contact support or fill in a form.

### 3.3 Demographic, education and career data
Age band and school/college year; institution; academic stream, subjects, grades or scores you choose to share; work history and current role (for professionals); career goals, aspirations, constraints and preferences; and any other information you volunteer in your profile, assessments or sessions.

### 3.4 Psychometric assessment data — *sensitive*
Your **responses to our assessments** and the **scores, profiles and interpretations** derived from them (Big Five personality traits, RIASEC interest codes, aptitude results). These reveal personality and interest characteristics, so we treat them as sensitive and only use them to generate your guidance, as described in section 4.

### 3.5 Session recordings, transcripts and notes — *sensitive*
When you attend a live video or voice counselling session, we record it and process it as follows, **with notice and consent at the point of the session**:

- the **audio and/or video recording** of the session;
- an **AI-generated transcript** of what was said; and
- **counsellor notes** taken during or after the session.

Your counselling session is a two-way conversation, so a recording will also capture your **counsellor or expert**, and anyone else you choose to bring into the session (for example, a parent). You keep access to your report, transcript and video in your client portal. Session recording is discussed further in section 6 and in the separate session-recording/informed-consent notice we show you before a session starts. **US note:** many US states require the consent of **all parties** to record a call — we obtain that consent on-screen before recording begins.

### 3.6 AI career-coach content — *may include sensitive content*
The **text messages** you send to, and receive from, our AI coach, and the **audio and transcript** of your live-voice interactions with it. Because you decide what you type or say, these may include sensitive details about you. We use them to answer you and to improve the coach, as described in section 4, and we apply the AI-specific safeguards in section 5.

### 3.7 Payment data
When you buy a package, assessment or session, payment is handled by our payment processor (Razorpay in India; card processors in the US). We receive **transaction metadata** — amount, currency, date/time, order and payment reference IDs, and a success/failure status. **We do not collect or store your full card number, CVV or UPI PIN;** those are handled directly by the processor on its PCI-DSS-compliant systems. We may store limited billing details (e.g. name, email, and for GST/tax purposes any invoicing details you provide).

### 3.8 Device, cookie and usage data
IP address; device and browser type; operating system; approximate location derived from IP; language settings; the pages and features you use, and how you use them; referral URLs; and identifiers set by cookies and similar technologies. See section 10 and our Cookie Policy.

### 3.9 Counsellor / expert background-verification data — *sensitive, applies only to practitioners*
If you apply to join us as a **counsellor ("navigator")** or **domain expert**, we collect what we need to verify and pay you: professional qualifications, certifications and CV; references; bank/UPI payout details; tax identifiers (e.g. PAN, GST) [CONFIRM]; and, for identity and background verification, **government-issued identity documents** and the results of any background checks. We collect government IDs only for verification and legal/tax compliance, restrict access to them, and retain them only as long as needed (section 7).

### 3.10 B2B contact data
For school, college and organisation clients, we collect business-contact details of the individuals we deal with (name, work email, phone, role) and information needed to deliver and administer the engagement.

### 3.11 Data we do not intentionally collect
We do not seek out information about your health, religion, caste, sexual orientation, biometric identifiers or political views. If such information surfaces (for example, because you mention it in a session or to the AI coach), we treat it as sensitive and handle it under this policy, but we do not ask for it and we discourage you from sharing more sensitive detail than the guidance needs.

## 5. How we use AI, and the safeguards that apply

The AI career coach (text and live voice) and parts of our report generation rely on AI models, some of which are operated by our sub-processors (section 6). To deliver these features:

- your prompts, messages and the relevant context (which may include assessment results and profile information) are sent to the AI provider to generate a response;
- for the **live voice** coach, your speech is converted to text and processed in real time;
- outputs are **guidance only** and can be incomplete or wrong — please use judgement and speak to a human counsellor for important decisions.

**Safeguards.** We instruct our AI sub-processors, by contract, **not to use your content to train their general models** and to process it only to provide the service to us [CONFIRM this is reflected in each provider agreement]. We minimise the personal data we send, apply access controls, and monitor for misuse. You can raise any concern about an AI output through our grievance channel (section 11). We do not use AI to make final, legally significant decisions about you without human involvement (section 4.2).

## 8. Your rights and how to exercise them

You have meaningful rights over your personal data. The exact rights depend on where you live, so we set out the India position first, then the US/California position, then a short EU/UK note. **To exercise any right**, contact us at grievance@setmycareer.com [CONFIRM] or use the contact details in section 12; you can also use the in-app privacy controls where available. We will verify your identity before acting on a request, respond within the timelines the law sets, and will not charge you unless the law allows it (for example, for manifestly excessive or repetitive requests).

### 8.1 India (DPDP Act, 2023)

As a **Data Principal**, you have the right to:

- **Access** — obtain a summary of the personal data we process about you and of our processing activities, and the identities of the Data Fiduciaries/processors with whom we have shared it.
- **Correction, completion and updating** — have inaccurate or incomplete data corrected or completed.
- **Erasure** — request deletion of your personal data where it is no longer needed for the purpose it was collected for or where you withdraw consent, unless we must keep it to comply with the law.
- **Withdraw consent** — withdraw any consent you have given, at any time, as easily as you gave it. Withdrawing consent does not affect processing already carried out, and may mean we can no longer provide some Services.
- **Grievance redressal** — have your grievance addressed by us in the first instance (section 11) before escalating.
- **Nominate** — nominate another individual to exercise your rights in the event of your death or incapacity.

You also have the right to **escalate to the Data Protection Board of India** if you are not satisfied with how we handle your grievance (section 11). We will act on verified requests within the timelines prescribed under the DPDP Act and its rules.

**Consent Manager.** The DPDP Act allows you to give, manage, review and withdraw your consent through a registered **Consent Manager**. Where a Consent Manager is available and you choose to use one, we will honour consent signals received through it [CONFIRM operational readiness].

### 8.2 United States (California residents — CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have the right to:

- **Know / Access** — request that we disclose the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you, the categories of sources, the business or commercial purpose for collecting it, and the categories of third parties we disclose it to.
- **Delete** — request that we delete personal information we collected from you, subject to legal exceptions.
- **Correct** — request that we correct inaccurate personal information.
- **Opt out of "sale" or "sharing"** — **we do not sell your personal information, and we do not "share" it for cross-context behavioural advertising** as those terms are defined under the CCPA/CPRA. Because we do not sell or share, there is nothing to opt out of; if this ever changes, we will provide a **"Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information"** link and honour opt-out preference signals such as **Global Privacy Control (GPC)**.
- **Limit the use of Sensitive Personal Information (SPI)** — we use SPI (such as your assessment results and session content) only for the purposes of providing the Services you request and other purposes permitted without a right to limit; we do not use SPI to infer characteristics about you for purposes that would trigger the right to limit.
- **Non-discrimination** — we will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights (for example, by denying Services or charging different prices), except where a difference is reasonably related to the value of the data, as permitted by law.
- **Authorised agent** — you may use an authorised agent to submit requests on your behalf; we will ask for proof of authorisation and may still verify your identity directly.

We will confirm receipt of your request within 10 business days and respond within **45 calendar days** (extendable by a further 45 days where reasonably necessary, with notice). You may make a verifiable request to know or delete **twice in a 12-month period** at no charge.

**"Shine the Light" (California Civil Code § 1798.83).** We do not disclose personal information to third parties for their own direct-marketing purposes.

### 8.3 Other US states (Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, Texas and more)
If you are a resident of a US state with a comprehensive privacy law (for example, **Virginia VCDPA, Colorado CPA, Connecticut CTDPA, Texas TDPSA** and others as they come into force), you generally have similar rights to **access, correct, delete and obtain a portable copy** of your personal data, to **opt out** of targeted advertising, sale and certain profiling, and to **appeal** a decision on your request. To exercise these rights, contact us as described above; if we decline your request you may appeal by replying to our decision, and you may contact your state Attorney General if you have concerns.

## 9. Children and minors

Career guidance for students is central to what we do, so we knowingly work with **minors** — but only with appropriate consent and safeguards.

### 9.1 India (DPDP Act, 2023) — under-18s
Under the DPDP Act, anyone **under 18** is a child. Before processing a child's personal data, we obtain **verifiable consent from a parent or lawful guardian**. We do not undertake tracking, behavioural monitoring or targeted advertising directed at children, and we do not process a child's data in a way likely to cause a detrimental effect on their wellbeing. A parent/guardian may exercise the child's rights and may withdraw consent at any time.

### 9.2 United States (COPPA) — under-13s
Under the US **Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)**, we obtain **verifiable parental consent** before collecting personal information from a child **under 13**, give parents the ability to review and delete their child's information and to refuse further collection, and collect only what is reasonably necessary for the child to use the Services. For US minors aged 13–17, we apply age-appropriate protections consistent with applicable state laws.

### 9.3 How verifiable parental consent works here
Depending on jurisdiction and the sensitivity of the activity, we verify parental consent using methods such as a consent step completed by the parent/guardian through a verified parent account, confirmation via a verified email/phone combined with a follow-up step, a signed consent form, or a payment-instrument check [CONFIRM the exact method(s) implemented]. We keep a record of the consent given.

### 9.4 Contact for parents/guardians
Parents and guardians can review, correct or delete their child's information, or withdraw consent, by contacting grievance@setmycareer.com [CONFIRM]. If we learn we have collected a child's data without the required consent, we will delete it promptly.

## 12. How we protect your data (security)

We implement reasonable technical and organisational security measures appropriate to the sensitivity of the data, including: encryption of data in transit (TLS) and encryption of sensitive data at rest; hashing of passwords; role-based access controls and the principle of least privilege; restricted access to session recordings, transcripts and government IDs; network and application security controls; logging and monitoring; vendor due-diligence and data-processing agreements with our sub-processors; and staff confidentiality obligations. In India, we maintain reasonable security practices consistent with the **IT Act, 2000** and the **SPDI Rules, 2011**, and we align with the security expectations of the DPDP Act.

No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security. If a personal-data breach occurs that is likely to affect you, we will notify you and the relevant authorities (including the **Data Protection Board of India** and, where applicable, US and EU/UK regulators) as and when the law requires. Please help protect your account by keeping your password confidential and telling us promptly about any suspected unauthorised use.
