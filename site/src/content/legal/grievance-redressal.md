If something has gone wrong — a service issue, a billing dispute, unwanted content, or a concern about how we handle your personal data — this policy tells you exactly whom to contact, what to expect, and how long it should take. We would rather hear from you and fix it than have you stay unhappy.

This Policy applies to everyone who uses SetMyCareer, including:

## 2. What you can raise a grievance about

You can contact us about any concern, including:

- **Service quality** — a counselling session, assessment, AI career coach interaction, or career report that fell short.
- **Billing and payments** — an incorrect charge, a refund, a failed transaction, or a cancellation.
- **Content** — anything on the platform you believe is unlawful, harmful, infringing, misleading, or otherwise objectionable, or content about you that you want removed.
- **Your personal data** — how we collect, use, store, share or retain it; a request to access, correct, update, erase or export your data; a request to withdraw consent; or a concern about a data breach.
- **Counsellors, experts or other users** — conduct, professionalism, or safety concerns.
- **Accessibility, discrimination or fair treatment.**
- **Anything else** you are not satisfied with.

## 4. How to raise a grievance

Please give us as much of the following as you can, so we can resolve your issue quickly:

1. Your **name** and preferred contact details (email and/or phone).
2. The **account, order, session or report** the grievance relates to (an order ID, booking reference or registered email helps).
3. A clear **description** of the problem, including relevant dates.
4. Any **screenshots, documents or evidence**.
5. **What outcome you are seeking** (for example: a refund, a correction, content removal, or erasure of your data).

You can reach us through any of these channels:

- **Email:** grievance@setmycareer.com [CONFIRM] (for grievances and data-protection requests), or info@setmycareer.com [CONFIRM] (for general support).
- **In-app / website:** the "Help" or "Contact / Report a concern" option in the SetMyCareer app or on setmycareer.com.
- **Phone:** +91-9108510058, Monday–Sunday, 09:00–20:00 IST.
- **Post:** to the registered office address in Section 3.

> **If you are raising a grievance on behalf of a minor**, please tell us that you are the parent or lawful guardian, and we may ask you to verify that relationship before we act (see Section 8).

## 5. Our staged process and timelines

We follow a clear, staged process. The **statutory resolution period depends on the type of grievance** — content grievances under the IT Rules, consumer grievances, and data-protection requests each have their own timeline, set out in Section 6.

### Stage 1 — Acknowledgement

We will **acknowledge every grievance within 24 to 48 hours** of receiving it, and give you a reference/ticket number. Acknowledgement means we have logged your complaint and assigned it — not that it is resolved.

### Stage 2 — Review and resolution

The Grievance Officer (or a trained team member under the Officer's supervision) will investigate, may contact you for more information, and will work to resolve your grievance **within the applicable statutory period** in Section 6. We will keep you informed of progress and tell you the outcome and the reasons for it.

If we genuinely need more time (for example, a complex investigation or a dependency on a third-party service provider), we will tell you before the deadline, explain why, and give you a revised timeline. We will not use this to delay unreasonably.

### Stage 3 — Internal escalation

If you are not satisfied with the outcome, you may ask us to escalate. A senior reviewer — independent of the person who first handled your grievance — will re-examine it. Please reply to your grievance email (keeping the reference number) and say you wish to escalate.

### Stage 4 — External escalation

If, after our internal process, you remain unsatisfied, you have the right to escalate to the relevant regulator or forum. Section 7 (India) and Section 9 (United States) set out those routes. Using our internal process first is encouraged but does not take away any legal right you have to approach a regulator, ombudsman or court directly.

## 7. Escalation in India

### 7.1 Data Protection Board of India (DPDP Act, 2023)

If your grievance concerns your **personal data** and you are not satisfied after (a) using our internal grievance process and (b) exhausting the response from our Data Protection Officer, you may lodge a complaint with the **Data Protection Board of India** in the manner prescribed under the DPDP Act, 2023 [CONFIRM: Board's official complaint channel/portal once operational]. Please raise the matter with us first — the DPDP Act expects you to have approached us before escalating to the Board.

### 7.2 Consumer forums and the National Consumer Helpline

If your grievance is a **consumer** matter (service quality, billing, refunds, misleading conduct) and remains unresolved, you may:

- Call the **National Consumer Helpline** on **1915**, or use the **e-Daakhil** portal (edaakhil.nic.in) to file a consumer complaint online; and/or
- Approach the appropriate **District, State or National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission**, depending on the value of your claim.

### 7.3 Other authorities

- For **unsolicited marketing** calls or messages, you may register on / report to the DND (Do Not Disturb) framework under the applicable **TRAI** regulations, in addition to telling us to stop (see our Privacy Policy).
- Nothing in this Policy limits your right to approach a court of competent jurisdiction.

## 9. United States — how US users raise and escalate grievances

US users can use every channel in Sections 3–4. In addition, the following applies.

### 9.1 California residents (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have the right to **know, access, correct and delete** your personal information, to **opt out of the "sale" or "sharing"** of your personal information, to **limit the use of your Sensitive Personal Information**, and to **not be discriminated against** for exercising these rights.

- **How to submit a request:** email grievance@setmycareer.com [CONFIRM] (or info@setmycareer.com [CONFIRM]) with "California Privacy Request" in the subject line, or use the in-app privacy request option. To opt out of sale/sharing, use the **"Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information"** link where offered.
- **Authorised agents:** you may use an authorised agent to submit a request; we may ask the agent for proof of authorisation and may ask you to verify your identity directly.
- **Our timeline:** we will confirm receipt **within 10 business days** and respond substantively **within 45 calendar days**, extendable once by a further **45 days** where reasonably necessary, in which case we will tell you.
- **Appeal:** if we decline your request, you may ask us to reconsider by replying to our decision. Where a state law (see 9.2) gives you a formal appeal right, we will honour it.
- **Escalation:** you may complain to the **California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA)** (cppa.ca.gov) or the **California Office of the Attorney General** (oag.ca.gov/privacy).

### 9.2 Other US state privacy laws

If you are a resident of Virginia (VCDPA), Colorado (CPA), Connecticut, Texas or another US state with a comprehensive privacy law, you have broadly similar rights (access, correction, deletion, portability, and opt-out of targeted advertising, sale and certain profiling), together with a **right to appeal** a refusal. Use the same channels above; we will apply the timelines and appeal rights your state law requires, and you may escalate to your **State Attorney General** if you are unsatisfied.

### 9.3 Children (COPPA)

For children under 13 in the US, a parent or guardian may review, correct or delete the child's personal information and withdraw consent by contacting us at grievance@setmycareer.com [CONFIRM]. We may verify the parent/guardian relationship before acting.

### 9.4 FTC

US consumers may also report concerns about unfair or deceptive practices to the **US Federal Trade Commission** at reportfraud.ftc.gov.
