# Quiet Mechanics
### An algorithmic philosophy for the SetMyCareer student-journey artworks

**Quiet Mechanics** is a movement of instrument-grade generative systems: six small,
living machines that behave like museum installations rather than website
illustrations. Each piece is a physical metaphor for one early career decision —
a portrait that resolves under attention, a stream that splits around an
obstruction, a bow that answers a steady hand, a puzzle that knows where it
belongs, pendulums that find a shared rhythm, a needle that returns to true
north. The metaphor is never written down; it is *felt*, the way a jazz quote is
felt — embedded in the parameters, not announced in a caption.

The cursor is a **force, never a button**. Every interaction is continuous:
fields, springs, impulses, drag coefficients. Nothing tweens; everything
integrates. Motion comes from semi-implicit integration of real quantities —
position, velocity, angular momentum, energy — with damping constants tuned by
hand until the systems feel *heavy and inevitable*. Overshoot is allowed because
mass is real; jitter is not, because craftsmanship is. Where per-pixel work is
required (dissolution, flow), the GPU does it in GLSL; where line-work is
required, the canvas draws only hairlines and lets white space carry the
composition.

The palette is a discipline, not a decoration: paper, ink at three opacities,
and a single purple — the assisted path — spent like a rationed accent on the
one element in each system that represents *the right choice*. Grain is present
but nearly subliminal. Type never enters the canvas. Each system is legible at
rest (a reduced-motion still must compose beautifully), rewards attention within
two seconds of contact, and never punishes the visitor — every state decays
gracefully back to equilibrium when the hand withdraws.

Every algorithm here must read as the product of countless refinement passes by
someone at the absolute top of the field: preallocated pools, zero per-frame
allocation, capped device-pixel ratios, IntersectionObserver-gated loops so only
the system in view spends energy, and full teardown on unmount. Sixty frames per
second is a contract, not an aspiration. The mark of the movement is restraint
under the surface of sophistication — a meticulously crafted mechanism whose
complexity is invisible and whose behaviour is unmistakably *alive*.

Seeded variation lives in the environment, not the physics: where a stone sits,
where a leaf drifts, the exact lengths of the pendulum cables. The physics is
deterministic law; the world it acts on is gently random. That is the emergent
signature of Quiet Mechanics — order you can lean on, chance you can notice.
